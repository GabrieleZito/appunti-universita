# Integrali di funzioni razionali

Partiamo analizzando $\large\int\frac{1}{ax^2+bx+c}dx$. Vi sono 3 casi:

1. $b^2-4ac>0:\ ax^2+bx+c=0$ ha 2 radici reali distinte. Siano esse $x_1$ e $x_2 \Rightarrow ax^2+bx+c=a(x-x_1)(x-x_2).$
    
    Si spezza
    
    $$
    \frac{1}{ax^2+bx+c}=\frac{1}{a(x-x_1)(x-x_2)}=\frac{1}{a}\bigg(\frac{A}{x-x_1}+\frac{B}{x-x_2}\bigg)
    $$
    
    calcolando $A$ e $B$.
    
    Di conseguenza
    
    $$
    \int\frac{1}{ax^2+bx+c}dx=\frac{1}{a}\bigg(\int\frac{A}{x-x_1}dx+\int\frac B {x-x_2}dx\bigg)=\frac 1 a\bigg(A\lg|x-x_1|+B\lg|x-x_2|\bigg)
    $$
    
    - Esempio
        
        $$
        \int\frac 1{x^2-1}dx=\int\frac 1{(x+1)(x-1)}dx.\\\text{ Pongo }\frac{1}{(x+1)(x-1)}=\frac A{(x+1)}+\frac B{(x-1)}=
        $$
        
        $$
        =\frac{A(x-1)+B(x+1)}{(x+1)(x-1)}=\frac{Ax-A+Bx+B}{(x+1)(x-1)}=\frac{(A+B)x-A+B}{(x+1)(x-1)}\Rightarrow
        \begin{cases}
        A+B=0\\-A+B=1
        \end{cases}\Rightarrow
        \begin{cases}
        A=-B\\B+B=1
        \end{cases}\Rightarrow
        \begin{cases}
        A=-\frac 12\\B=\frac12
        \end{cases}\Rightarrow
        $$
        
        $$
        \Rightarrow\frac{A}{x+1}+\frac{B}{x-1}=\frac{-\frac12}{x+1}+\frac{\frac12}{x-1}\Rightarrow
        \int\frac 1{x^2-1}dx=-\frac12\int\frac{1}{x+1}dx+\frac12\int\frac{1}{x-1}dx=
        -\frac12\lg|x+1|+\frac12\lg|x-1|+c=\frac12\lg\bigg|\frac{x-1}{x+1}\bigg|+c
        $$
    
2. $b^2-4ac=0:$   $ax^2+bx+c=0$   ha 1 radice con molteplicità 2. 
    
    Sia essa $x_1\Rightarrow ax^2+bx+c=a(x-x_1)^2=\int\frac 1 {a(x-x_1)^2}dx=\frac 1 a\int\frac{1}{(x-x_1)^2}dx=\frac{1}{a}\int(x-x_1)^{-2}dx=\frac 1 a\bigg(-\frac 1 {x-x_1}\bigg)+c$
    
3. $b^2-4ac<0:$   $ax^2+bx+c=0$ non ammette radici reali  $\Rightarrow ax^2+bx+c=a\bigg(x^2+\frac bax+\frac ca\bigg)=a\bigg(x^2+\frac bax+\frac{b^2}{4a^2}-\frac{b^2}{4a^2}+\frac ca\bigg)=a\bigg(\bigg(x+\frac{b}{2a}\bigg)^2+\frac ca-\frac{b^2}{4a^2}\bigg)=a\bigg(\bigg(x+\frac{b}{2a}\bigg)^2+\frac {4ac+b^2}{4a^2}\bigg)$
    Si mette in evidenza $\frac{4ac-b^2}{4a^2}>0$ e si risolve facendo comparire un $\arctan$.
    
- Esempio
    
    $$
    \int\frac{1}{x^2+2x+2}dx\Rightarrow x^2+2x+2=(x^2+1)^2+1\Rightarrow\int\frac{1}{(x^2+1)^2+1}d(x+1)=\arctan(x+1)+c
    $$
    
    ---
    
    $$
    \int\frac{1}{x^2+x+2}dx\Rightarrow x^2+x+2=\bigg(x^2+2\frac12x+\frac14\bigg)-\frac14+2=\\
    $$
    
    $$
    =
    \bigg(x+\frac12\bigg)^2+\frac74=\bigg(\frac{2x+1}2\bigg)^2+\frac74=\frac{(2x+1)^2}4+\frac74=\frac{(2x+1)^2+7}{4}\Rightarrow\\
    $$
    
    $$
    \Rightarrow
    \int\frac1{x^2+x+2}dx=\int\frac{1}{\frac{(2x+1)^2+7}{4}}dx=4\int\frac{1}{(2x+1)^2+7}dx=\\
    $$
    
    $$
    =
    4\int\frac{1}{7\bigg(\frac{(2x+1)^2}{7}+1\bigg)}=\frac47\int\frac1{\frac{(2x+1)^2}{7}+1}dx=\frac47\int\frac{1}{(\frac{2x+1}{\sqrt7})^2+1}dx=\\
    $$
    
    $$
    =
    \frac47\int\frac1{(\frac{2}{\sqrt7}x+\frac1{\sqrt7})^2+1}dx\Rightarrow\\\Rightarrow t=\frac{2}{\sqrt7}x+\frac1{\sqrt7}\Rightarrow dt=\frac2{\sqrt7}dx\Rightarrow dx=\frac{\sqrt7}2dt\Rightarrow
    $$
    
    $$
    \Rightarrow\frac47\int\frac{1}{t^2+1}\frac{\sqrt7}{2}dt=\frac{2\sqrt7}{7}\int\frac{1}{t^2+1}dt=\frac{2\sqrt7}{7}\arctan t+c=\frac{2\sqrt7}{7}\arctan(\frac2{\sqrt7}x+\frac1{\sqrt7})+c
    $$
    

### Integrali di frazioni di polinomi

Consideriamo ora $\int\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}dx$. Se $\deg P(x)\ge\deg Q(x),$  dividiamo $P(x)$ per $Q(x)$:

$P(x)=Q(x)H(x)+R(x),$ con $R(x)=0$ oppure $\deg R(x)<\deg Q(x)\Rightarrow\int\frac{P(x)}{Q(x)}dx=\int\frac{Q(x)H(x)+R(x)}{Q(x)}dx=\int\bigg( H(x)+\frac{R(x)}{Q(x)}\bigg)dx=\\=\int H(x)dx+\int\frac{R(x)}{Q(x)}dx$


Ma $H(x)$ è un integrale immediato, dunque rimane da risolvere $\int\frac{R(x)}{Q(x)}dx$ 

Decomponiamo $Q(x)=(x-\alpha_1)^{n_1}(x-\alpha_2)^{n_2}\cdots(ax^2+bx+c)^{m_1}\cdots$

$$
\text{allora }\frac{R(x)}{Q(x)}=\frac{A}{x-\alpha_1}+\frac{B}{x-\alpha_2}+\cdots+\frac{Ex+F}{ax^2+bx+c}+D\bigg(\frac{G(x)}{(x-\alpha_1)^{n_1-1}(x-\alpha_2)^{n_2-1}\cdots(ax^2+bx+c)^{m_1-1}\cdots}\bigg)
$$

dove $G(x)=A_0+A_1x+\cdots+A_kx^k$ e $k$ il grado del polinomio a numeratore meno 1.

- Esempi
    
    $$\begin{gathered}
	\int\frac{3x+2}{x(x^2+1)}\Rightarrow \frac{3x+2}{x(x^2+1)}=\frac Ax+\frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1}=\frac{Ax^2+A+Bx^2+Cx}{x(x^2+1)}=\\\ \\=\frac{(A+B)x^2+Cx+A}{x(x^2+1)}\Rightarrow\\\ \\\Rightarrow
    \begin{cases}
    A+B=0\\C=3\\A=2
    \end{cases}\Rightarrow
    \begin{cases}
    A=2\\B=-2\\C=3
    \end{cases}\Rightarrow\\\ \\\Rightarrow\int\frac{3x+2}{x(x^2+1)}dx=\int\frac2xdx+\int\frac{-2x+3}{x^2+1}dx=2\lg|x|-\int\frac{2x-3}{x^2+1}dx=\\\ \\=
    2\lg|x|-\int\frac{2x}{x^2+1}dx+\int\frac{3}{x^2+1}dx=\\=2\lg|x|-\lg|x^2+1|+3\arctan x+c
    
	\end{gathered}
    $$
    
    ---
    
    $$
    \int\frac{1}{(x-1)^2x^3}dx\Rightarrow\frac{1}{(x-1)^2x^3}=\frac{A}{x-1}+\frac Bx+D\bigg(\frac{Cx^2+Ex+F}{(x-1)x^2}\bigg)=
    $$
    
    $$
    =\frac{Ax+Bx-B}{x(x-1)}+\frac{(2Cx+E)(x-1)x^2-(Cx^2+Ex+F)(3x^2-2x)}{(x-1)^2x^4}=
    $$
    
    $$
    \frac{(Ax+Bx-B)(x^4-x^3)+(2Cx^3+Ex^2)(x-1)-(3Cx^4-2Cx^3+3Ex^3-2Ex^2+3Fx^2-2Fx)}{(x-1)^2x^4}=
    $$
    
    $$
    \frac{Ax^5-Ax^4+Bx^5-Bx^4-Bx^4+Bx^3+2Cx^4-2Cx^3+Ex^3-Ex^2-3Cx^4+2Cx^3-3Ex^3+2Ex^2-3Fx^2+2Fx}{(x-1)^2x^4}=
    $$
    
    $$
    =\frac{(A+B)x^5+(-A-2B-C)x^4+(B-2E)x^3+(E-3F)x^2+2Fx}{(x-1)^2x^4}=
    $$
    
    $$
    =\frac{(A+B)x^4+(-A-2B-C)x^3+(B-2E)x^2+(E-3F)x+2F}{(x-1)^2x^3}\Rightarrow
    $$
    
    $$
    \Rightarrow\begin{cases}
    A+B=0\\
    -A-2B-C=0\\
    B-2E=0\\
    E-3F=0\\
    2F=1
    \end{cases}\Rightarrow
    \begin{cases}
    F=\frac12\\
    E=\frac32\\
    B=3\\A=-3\\C=-3
    \end{cases}\Rightarrow
    $$
    
    $$
    \Rightarrow\int\frac{-3}{x-1}dx+\int\frac3xdx+\int D\bigg(\frac{-3x^2+\frac32x+\frac12}{(x-1)x^2}\bigg)dx=
    -3\lg|x-1|+3\lg|x|-\frac{-3x^2+\frac32x+\frac12}{(x-1)x^2}+c
    $$
